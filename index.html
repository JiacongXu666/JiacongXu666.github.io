Visible-Infrared-Datasets

1. Paired

LLVIP Dataset

Paper

Towards: Image Fusion, Low-light Pedestrian Detection, and Image-to-image Translation; Advantages: The RGB and Infrared images are strictly paired for space and time (after registration), and it contains night scenario and high-resolution infrared images; Disadvantages: The camera is placed at a fixed location on the street, so it is single-view and video-based.

M3FD Dataset

Paper

Towards: Image Fusion, Infrared-visible Object Detection; Advantages: The RGB and Infrared images are strictly paired for space and time (after registration), and it contains multiple scenarioes (smoogy, hash weather…) and relatively high-resolution images; Disadvantages: it is still single-view for each scene.

DUT-VTUAV Dataset (may be usable)

Paper

Towards: Single Object Tracking; Advantages: The RGB and Infrared images are paired for space and time (apply the alignment of initial frames to all the remaining frames), and the sensors are placed on a UAV to follow the object and captures multiple scenarioes (night, smoogy, windy weather…); Disadvantages: Even though the dataset is video-based, the UAV is following the object along the video and thus the dataset can be considered for video-based nerf such as this Paper.

KAIST Dataset

Paper

Towards: Image Fusion, Pedestrian Detection; Advantages: The RGB and Infrared images are strictly paired, and it contains multiple scenarioes (street, campus, night…); Disadvantages: low-resolution, the sensors are placed on a car and the data was collected when driving.

TNO Dataset

Website

Towards: Image Fusion; Advantages: The visible and Infrared images are strictly paired, contains multispectral (enhanced visual, near-infrared, and long-wave infrared or thermal) nighttime imagery of different military scenes; Disadvantages: low-resolution, single-view, small dataset

INO Videos Analytics Dataset

Website

Towards: Image Fusion, Detection, Segmentation; Advantages: The RGB and Infrared videos are strictly paired, very rich scenes and environments,; Disadvantages: video-based, low-resolution, single-view, few night images

OTCBVS OSU Color-Thermal Database

Website

Towards: Image Fusion; Advantages: The visible and Infrared images are strictly paired, contains multispectral (enhanced visual, near-infrared, and long-wave infrared or thermal) nighttime imagery of different military scenes; Disadvantages: low-resolution, single-view, small dataset

Multimodal-NeRF-Reading

1. Multimodal Neural Radiance Field

Paper

Method

Point Cloud Extraction

The relative transformation matrix can be estimated for the sets of 2D images (rgb, infrared). Project the points on each image to the 3D space by the matrix and depth (estimated by Depth Estimation Network), then we will have the point cloud for each modality.

Point Cloud Registration

Extract the features for both of the two point clouds by a feature extraction network and align two spaces by RANSAC.

Loss

Following PointNeRF.

Dataset

In addition to the RGB images, M2DGR also provides thermal-infrared images, which capture the 2D geometry information in greyscale images with infrared sensors. 165 infrared images along with 280 RGB images are selected.

Performance

More modalities will lead to better performance (compared with naive NeRF and PointNeRF).
